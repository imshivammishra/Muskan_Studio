About — The Designer

The Story
of Muskan

A life woven from the vibrant streets of Mumbai and the storied ateliers of Paris — where heritage meets vision, and every thread carries a story.

01 — Origins

Where Two
Worlds Converge

Paris Atelier, 2024

Muskan was born in the heart of Mumbai's Kala Ghoda district, where her mother — a textile restorer at the National Museum — surrounded her with the language of fabric before she could read.

By the age of fourteen, she was draping her grandmother's old saris into evening gowns, instinctively understanding how cloth moves, how it catches light, how it transforms the body beneath it. It was this early intuition — half craft, half poetry — that would come to define her entire approach to design.

After graduating at the top of her class from the National Institute of Fashion Technology in Delhi, Muskan earned a coveted scholarship to the École de la Chambre Syndicale de la Couture Parisienne — the legendary school that has trained the likes of Karl Lagerfeld and Thierry Mugler. In the quiet intensity of Parisian ateliers, she absorbed the meticulous discipline of haute couture: the thousands of hand-stitches in a single bodice, the engineering of a perfectly balanced silhouette, the patient pursuit of beauty in details invisible to everyone but the wearer.

But it was her roots that kept calling. In 2017, Muskan returned to Mumbai with a vision that would set her apart — to create a fashion house that was neither purely Western nor purely Indian, but something entirely new: a dialogue between two great sartorial traditions, spoken in the universal language of beauty.

She founded Muskan Studio from a small atelier in Le Marais, Paris, debuting with a collection titled "Rêverie" — a meditation on memory, place, and the invisible threads that connect us to the places we love. The collection was selected for Paris Fashion Week's official calendar, and the fashion world took notice.

The Hand of the Maker
02 — Craft

The Hand of the Maker

Every garment that leaves the Muskan Studio atelier carries the imprint of human hands. In an industry increasingly dominated by speed and automation, Muskan has made a deliberate choice to work slowly — to honour the artisans whose skills have been passed down through generations of Indian craftsmanship. Each collection takes between four and six months to produce, with some individual pieces requiring over three hundred hours of hand-embroidery alone. The studio employs a collective of master artisans based in Mumbai and Varanasi, many of whom come from families who have practised their craft for centuries. For Muskan, this is not nostalgia — it is the future of luxury.

Between Two Worlds
03 — Vision

Between Two Worlds

What sets Muskan apart in the landscape of contemporary fashion is her refusal to choose between cultures. Her work is a constant, evolving conversation between the restraint of Parisian couture and the exuberance of Indian textile traditions — between a perfectly cut black wool coat and a sari blouse hand-embroidered with zardozi gold thread. "I don't believe in fusion," Muskan has said in interviews. "Fusion implies compromise. What I create is a dialogue — each tradition speaking on its own terms, creating something that could only exist when they meet." This philosophy has earned her a devoted following among women who see fashion not as decoration, but as a form of cultural expression — and a roster of editorial features in Vogue Italia, Harper's Bazaar, and W Magazine.

04 — Philosophy

Three Pillars

01

Intentional Craft

Every stitch is a decision. Every seam, a commitment. We reject the disposable in favour of the enduring — creating garments that are built to be cherished across a lifetime, not a season.

02

Cultural Dialogue

True innovation lives at the intersection of traditions. We draw from the extraordinary depth of Indian artisanal heritage and the refinement of Parisian haute couture to create something entirely singular.

03

Quiet Power

Luxury should never announce itself. It should be felt — in the weight of the fabric, the grace of the drape, the intimate knowledge that something extraordinary was made just for you.
